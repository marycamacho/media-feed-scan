### ABOUT CIRDIa (≈500 words)
Cirdia is a Colorado-based Public Benefit Corporation pioneering **privacy-first wellness wearables** designed for women navigating midlife and beyond.  
Its dual-device system—the **Day Device (Contour)** and **Night Device (Noir)**—works together to help users understand patterns of recovery, stress, energy, and rest without exposing their personal data to centralized servers. All processing happens locally or through user-controlled encrypted storage.

Cirdia’s founding insight is that wellness data are among the most intimate forms of personal information. True health technology must therefore protect agency, not monetize attention.  
Where most consumer trackers gamify behavior or sell aggregated biometrics, Cirdia focuses on *functional wellness*—helping people build resilience, strength, and balance over decades rather than chasing daily streaks.  
Its design language and user experience are grounded in dignity and configurability: users can choose minimalist pattern feedback, detailed analytics, or guided insights depending on their goals and comfort.

The company’s public-benefit mandate commits it to advancing wellness innovation that serves the public good.  
Its charter includes:  
- **Empowering user agency** through transparent data practices and opt-in sharing.  
- **Representing under-served groups**—especially women 45+—in design, research, and leadership.  
- **Enabling community collaboration** so user data, if shared, contributes to open research under user-controlled terms.  
- **Building ethical infrastructure** for long-term longevity studies and personalized wellness models.

Cirdia intends an eventual **exit to community**, transitioning toward employee-ownership and cooperative stakeholder governance.  
This ensures that the company’s values—privacy, equity, and inclusion—remain structurally protected even after growth or liquidity events.  
Investors participate through capped-return, revenue-based instruments that align financial outcomes with mission durability rather than speculative exit.

The brand’s voice rejects both tech utopianism and fear-based wellness marketing.  
It meets users where they are—acknowledging complexity, honoring autonomy, and celebrating functional strength across life stages.  
Cirdia’s broader goal is to redefine wellness technology as a form of civic infrastructure: tools that respect privacy, foster self-knowledge, and strengthen communities.
